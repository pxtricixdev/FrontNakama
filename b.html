OCCUPATIONAL RISK ASSESSMENT

 

1.Occupational Risks for a Waiter

  • Overexertion: Proper posture is required when handling loads to prevent musculoskeletal injuries. Assess the weight before lifting and ask for help if necessary.
  • Impacts: Common in areas with furniture or kitchen objects that can cause frequent impacts to arms or legs.
  • Falls: Pay attention to slippery floors and clean spills quickly. Signal any risk situations immediately to prevent slips and trips.
  • Injuries from Sharp or Pointed Objects: Tools like scissors, razors, and sharpeners must be properly handled, stored, and maintained.
  • Burns: A major risk in kitchens, occurring from fires, splashes, and hot utensils.
  • Sudden Changes in Temperature: Be aware of heat in the environment and poor ventilation which can affect thermal comfort.
  • Contamination: Disinfection and cleaning are essential to prevent biological contamination such as fungi, bacteria, and viruses.
  • Stress: Caused by excessive workload, long workdays without breaks, and lack of motivation from superiors.

2.Risks for a Cook

  • Cuts and Punctures: Main risks come from handling manual utensils and auxiliary machines.
  • Falls or Slips: Occur due to poor floor conditions, being dirty, greasy, or wet.
  • Impacts and Falling Objects: Lack of order and organization in the kitchen increases risk.
  • Thermal Contact and Burns: Hot surfaces, container contents, and heat sources like stoves can cause severe burns.
  • Acoustic Contamination: Common due to simultaneous operation of devices and general kitchen noise.
  • Thermal Stress: Wear appropriate clothing for high or low temperatures to manage body heat.
  • Electrical Installations: Risk of accidents from using electrical appliances with wet hands or clothing.
  • Ergonomic Risks: Important to maintain correct postures and handle loads properly to avoid injuries.
  • Biological Risks: Continuous food handling can expose workers to harmful biological agents.
  • Kitchen Cleaning: Use of chemical products requires precautions to avoid health risks like burns or inhalation of vapors.

 

 

Preventive Measures for Cooks

  • Equipment and Tool Maintenance: Keep all tools and machines well-maintained and sharp. Disconnect them during cleaning or repairs.
  • Safety Measures in Machinery Use: Always use pushers and protections when operating machines.
  • Personal Protection: Wear appropriate personal protective equipment to prevent accidents.
  • Accident and Injury Prevention: Maintain good workspace organization and signal wet floors during and after cleaning.
  • Hygiene and Health: Enhance cleaning routines to manage exposure to harmful vapors and biological agents.
  • Chemical and Biological Risk Management: Use protective gear like gloves and masks when handling chemicals.

 

 

3.Occupational Risks for a Web Developer

  • Ergonomic Risks: Web developers often spend long hours in front of the computer, which can cause posture problems, visual fatigue, and musculoskeletal injuries. Proper ergonomics in the workplace, using ergonomic furniture and equipment, as well as taking breaks and performing stretching exercises are important to prevent injuries.
  • Visual Risks: Constant work in front of screens can cause visual fatigue, dry eyes, blurred vision, and other eye problems. It is advisable to take regular breaks, blink frequently, and adjust the lighting of the work environment to reduce visual strain.
  • Psychosocial Risks: Web development can be a stressful job, especially when facing tight deadlines, constant pressure, and client demands. Establishing a work-life balance and having a healthy work environment and emotional support are crucial.
  • Electrical Risks: Web developers work with electronic equipment and cables, which involves the risk of electric shocks. Ensuring that the equipment is properly grounded, using plugs and cables in good condition, and avoiding contact with liquids near electrical devices are essential safety practices.
  • Information Security Risks: Web developers are exposed to information security risks such as hacker attacks, data theft, and computer viruses. Having adequate security measures like the use of secure passwords, up-to-date antivirus software, and regular backups to protect information and systems is essential.
  • Sedentary Risks: Web development work is usually sedentary, which can lead to health problems such as overweight, obesity, and cardiovascular diseases. Engaging in regular physical activity, maintaining a balanced diet, and avoiding sedentary behavior during work hours are important health measures.

 

 

Preventive Measures for Web Developers

  • Risk Assessments: Identify potential hazards and assess the risks associated with web developer work.
  • Ergonomic Equipment: Ensure that web developers have adjustable chairs and tables, as well as ergonomic monitors and keyboards to prevent musculoskeletal injuries.
  • Regular Breaks: Encourage web developers to take regular breaks to avoid visual fatigue and physical stress.
  • Safety Training: Train web developers in safe work practices, such as the proper use of equipment and correct posture.
  • Information Security Measures: Protect company systems and data using firewalls, antivirus, and other information security measures.
  • Regular Backups: Ensure that data and web developer projects are regularly backed up to prevent the loss of important information.
  • Stress Awareness: Provide support and resources to help web developers manage work-related stress, such as wellness programs and access to mental health services.
  • Flexible Work Policies: Allow flexible work schedules and remote work options to reduce fatigue and stress associated with long work hours.
  • Open Communication: Establish open communication channels and encourage web developers to report any work-related problems or risks they encounter.

 

 

MAIN FUNCTIONS OF THE COMPANY COMMITTEE

 

 

  1. Worker Representation: The Company Committee serves as the voice of the employees to management, communicating concerns, suggestions, and needs to improve the work environment. This includes issues ranging from wages and schedules to professional development and organizational climate.
  2. Collective Bargaining: Participates in the negotiation of collective agreements that establish working conditions, including wages, working hours, leaves, and other benefits. The goal is to ensure fair and competitive conditions that benefit both the company and its employees.
  3. Information and Consultation: Receives and analyzes information about the financial situation and future strategies of the company. This allows the Committee to participate in strategic planning and ensure that proposed changes do not harm the interests of the workers.
  4. Conflict Resolution: Acts as a mediator in labor disputes, seeking fair and equitable solutions that maintain harmony and mutual respect within the team.
  5. Monitoring Compliance with Labor Laws: Ensures that the company complies with current labor legislation, supervising hiring practices, working conditions, safety and health, and equality of treatment among all employees.
  6. Health and Safety at Work: Promotes a safe work environment through the constant evaluation of risks and the organization of training on safe work practices, equipment handling, and emergencies.
